
COMMON MEDICATIONS

ANALGESICS (Pain Relievers)
Paracetamol (Acetaminophen): 500-1000mg every 4-6 hours. Max 4g/day. Safe for most patients.
Caution: Liver toxicity in overdose or with alcohol use.
Ibuprofen (NSAID): 200-400mg every 4-6 hours. Take with food.
Caution: Avoid in kidney disease, peptic ulcer, pregnancy (3rd trimester), heart disease.
Aspirin: 75mg (cardioprotective), 300-600mg (pain/fever). Avoid in under 16 years old.

ANTIBIOTICS (Prescription Only)
Amoxicillin: Common bacterial infections. 250-500mg three times daily. 7-10 day course.
Azithromycin: Respiratory infections. 500mg day 1, then 250mg for 4 days.
Metronidazole: Anaerobic infections, H.pylori, dental infections. Avoid alcohol.
NOTE: Always complete the full antibiotic course. Do not self-prescribe antibiotics.

ANTIHYPERTENSIVES
Amlodipine (Calcium Channel Blocker): 5-10mg once daily. Side effect: ankle swelling.
Enalapril/Lisinopril (ACE Inhibitor): 5-40mg daily. Side effect: dry cough.
Losartan (ARB): 50-100mg daily. Alternative if ACE inhibitor cough occurs.
Metoprolol (Beta Blocker): 25-200mg daily. Do not stop suddenly. Side effect: fatigue.

ANTIDIABETICS
Metformin: First-line for Type 2 DM. 500-2000mg daily. Take with meals. Side effect: GI upset.
Insulin: Various types (rapid, short, intermediate, long-acting). Requires proper training.
