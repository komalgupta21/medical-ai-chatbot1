
CHEST PAIN - DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
Cardiac causes: Angina, myocardial infarction (crushing pain, radiates to arm/jaw), pericarditis.
Pulmonary causes: Pulmonary embolism (sudden, with breathlessness), pneumonia, pleuritis.
GI causes: GERD (burning), esophageal spasm, peptic ulcer.
Musculoskeletal: Costochondritis (tender to touch), rib fracture.
RED FLAGS: Central crushing pain + sweating + nausea - CALL EMERGENCY immediately.

HEADACHE TYPES
Tension headache: Band-like pressure around head. Treated with OTC analgesics, rest.
Migraine: Pulsating, one-sided, with nausea/vomiting, light/sound sensitivity. Lasts 4-72 hours.
Cluster headache: Severe pain around one eye, occurring in clusters. Extremely painful.
RED FLAGS: Sudden severe headache, headache with fever and stiff neck, after head injury - seek emergency care.

ABDOMINAL PAIN LOCATIONS
Right upper quadrant: Liver, gallbladder issues (gallstones cause sharp pain after fatty meals).
Left upper quadrant: Stomach, spleen, pancreas.
Right lower quadrant: Appendicitis (starts central, moves to right - EMERGENCY if severe).
Left lower quadrant: Diverticulitis, IBS.

SHORTNESS OF BREATH
Acute: Asthma attack, pulmonary embolism, pneumothorax, heart failure, anaphylaxis.
Chronic: COPD, heart failure, anemia, obesity, anxiety.
